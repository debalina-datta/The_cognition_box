We are all familiar with statues of famous persons worldwide to honor their great deeds. But did you know that a similar monument exists in honor of lab mice as well? Yes, that’s right! The Monument to the laboratory mouse (Pamyatnik Laboratornoy Myshi) is situated in Akademgorodok, Novosibirsk, Russia, a facility of the Novosibirsk State University. The bronze sculpture of a mouse, unveiled on July 1, 2013, was built to honor the sacrifice of mice in research in science. Mice are used extensively for genetic engineering research, understanding the molecular basis of biological processes. and drug development. The sculpture was designed by artist Andrey Kharkevich and embodied by sculptor Alexei Agricolyansky. The statue represents a mouse with glasses on its nose, knitting the double helix of DNA with needles in its hands.
